Pamekasan's Culinary Delights

Exploring the Authentic Flavors from the Heart of Madura

Pamekasan is not only rich in culture but also a paradise for flavor seekers. Here are some of the legendary dishes you must taste to experience the true soul of Madurese cuisine.

Sate Lala' (Fly Satay)

Don't be fooled by the name; this satay is made from tiny, finely cut pieces of chicken or mutton. Marinated and grilled to perfection, each bite-sized skewer is packed with the rich flavor of Madura's signature peanut sauce.

Campor Lorjuk

A truly unique Pamekasan specialty. This savory dish combines rice cakes, vermicelli, and crispy fried dough with lorjuk (bamboo clams), all drenched in a rich, reddish broth that's bursting with local spices.

Kaldu Kokot (Trotter Broth)

A comforting and hearty soup made from slow-braised beef trotters (kokot). Cooked for hours with green beans and spices, the broth is rich and savory, with incredibly tender meat that falls off the bone.

Nasi Jejen

Pamekasan's quintessential breakfast, similar to nasi rames. It features a scoop of rice surrounded by flavorful sides, such as spicy braised beef (daging osik), egg in shrimp paste sauce, and crispy potato flakes.

Soto Ayam Keppo

A local legend from the Keppo area, this Madurese chicken soto offers a uniquely refreshing taste. Unlike the yellow, koya-topped soto found elsewhere, Soto Keppo features a light, clear broth that is savory yet delicately spiced, filled with tender free-range chicken and topped with a delightful crunch.

Tajin Madura (Madura Porridge)

A sweet and comforting porridge mix. A bowl typically contains smooth rice flour porridge, tapioca pearls, and glutinous rice balls, all drizzled with a generous amount of liquid palm sugar. Perfect for breakfast or dessert.
